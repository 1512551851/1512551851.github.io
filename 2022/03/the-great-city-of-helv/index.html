The Great City of Helv

2022年3月13日 发布在 随笔, 学习记录

 Suzhou was founded as the capital of the Wu Kingdom in the sixth century BC by King HeLv.In 514 BC, He Lv’s Prime Minister, Wu Zixu, was ordered to reconstruct the capital, which wascompleted in 508 BC.This was the beginning of the city as it stands today.

 Wu Zixu, who wasgood at strategy, believed that the first thing to do in stabilizing the country and governing the peoplewas to build a polis, so the first major thing was to create The Great City ofHelv.The planning andconstruction of The Great City of Helv was to realize the political intention of King Helv to breakthe Chu, control the Yue and dominate the feudal lords.

 The site of the city was carefully chosen and planned,requiring observation of the land,Detection of hydrology, extensive geological investigations, and comparison and selection.Finally,according to the rules of the sky and earth,Wu Zixu chose the proper site to build the city, whichwas 30 miles northeast of Gusu.After several years of hard work, a large number of people wererecruited to build it,which was finally completed with a forty-seven-mile circumference, and istoday known as the ancient city of Suzhou, which was located near the Yangtze River in the northand Taihu Lake in the west, and was surrounded by three rivers, five lakes and a vast expanse ofwater and plain.Therefore, the area had a great potential for growth, which was exactly what thestate Wu needed to flourish and govern.The Great City of Helv was built from the very beginningwith the idea that the city would be a good place to live, which was built with a thorough siteselection and construction plan, reflecting the scientific thinking of our ancestors. The reason whythe ancient city of Suzhou has stood for more than 2,500 years is that the site was chosen inaccordance with the laws of nature.

 The Great City of Helv, built by Wu Zixu, had eight gates on land and water，includingChangmen City Gate, Xumen City Gate, Panmen City Gate,Shemen City Gate, Loumen City Gate,Jiangmen City Gate, Pingmen City Gate and Qimen City Gate, which created the first well-plannedwater city model in the history of China. There were two pairs of water and land gates on each ofthe four sides of the city, which were determined by the flow of water from the river outside the city,so that water can be drawn into the city or drained out of it. It was interesting that the names of thegates of Suzhou were all single characters, which was unique in China. The ancient city gates ofSuzhou had simple and elegant names, but each had a specific origin and a profound meaning,reflecting their respective settings and characteristics, and containing a deep cultural background.Changmen City Gate building on the western wall was a symbol of the gate to heaven, so that thewind of Helv could pass through.Changmen City Gate was also known as the gate of breaking Chu.Shemen City Gate was set up to symbolise the gate of the earth.According to the Bagua, the Wustate is a dragon, so the southwest gate was named Panmen City Gate, while the southeastern gatewas named Shemen City Gate because the state of Yue belonged to the snake, so that to suppress it.The northern gates were called the Pingmen City Gate and Qimen City Gate respectively, naturallyto deter jin.

 Ancient Chinese philosophy took heaven, earth and man as a cosmic system, pursuing the unityof heaven, earth and man and the harmony and unity of all things in the universe.The Great City ofHelv was planned on the model of heaven and earth, and rich symbolic meaning was given to thetype, number, orientation and direction of the gates, as well as the decoration of dragons and snakeson the gates， so that the planning layout of the city and the shape of the buildings reflected thephilosophical idea of the unity of heaven, earth and man.
